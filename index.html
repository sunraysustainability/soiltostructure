Richard Daley is a whole-systems designer and builder focused on regenerative, human-scale systems. He has an interdisciplinary background in ecological design, green building, and plant-based systems.

Richard has been involved in community-based work for many years through his organization, Sunray Sustainability, collaborating with schools, nonprofits, and neighborhood initiatives across New York’s Capital District. These projects have helped build community self-reliance, grow food, plant trees, build green, teach valuable skills, and beyond.

In addition to his design/build practice and community work, Richard has been active in education for nearly two decades. He has taught in various settings from formal institutions to grassroots gatherings on a variety of subjects related to living well, and sustainably, on Earth.

Richard holds a degree in Green Building from the State University of New York, and his writing has appeared in Permaculture Design Magazine, Lion’s Roar Magazine, The Times Union, Resilience, and other publications.
