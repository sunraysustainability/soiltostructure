This is the personal website of Richard Daley, created to share independent work and offer consulting and services to those interested.

Grounded in practice and guided by a whole-systems approach to design and building, my work centers on regenerative, human-scale systems. Over the years I’ve collaborated on numerous projects and facilitated workshops in varied settings, from formal institutions to grassroots gatherings. I have a degree in Green Building from the State University of New York, and my writing has appeared in Permaculture Design Magazine, Lion’s Roar Magazine, Resilience, and other publications. What matters most to me are the relationships I’ve built and the collective work I’ve been part of—real, community-based efforts to build a better future.

Through my organization, Sunray Sustainability, I have collaborated with schools, nonprofits, and neighborhood initiatives across New York’s Capital District. These projects have helped build community self-reliance, grow food, plant trees, build green, teach valuable skills, and beyond.

I have also been active in education for nearly two decades. I have taught on a variety of subjects related to living well on Earth.
